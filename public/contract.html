1. RECITALS

  1. WHEREAS Swifter Pay (Pty) Ltd ("the Company") is a financial technology company that provides digital payment infrastructure, cross-border money transfer services, and related fintech solutions;
  2. WHEREAS Gustav Vermaas ("the Representative") possesses relevant experience and business relationships that may generate new paying customers for the Company;
  3. WHEREAS the Parties wish to enter into a commission-based arrangement whereby the Representative shall be compensated for introducing paying customers to the Company;
  4. NOW THEREFORE, the Parties agree to the terms and conditions set out in this Agreement.

2. DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

In this Agreement, unless the context indicates otherwise:

  1. "Paying Customer" means a customer introduced by the Representative who has: (a) signed a binding service agreement with the Company; (b) completed onboarding; and (c) made at least one (1) confirmed payment for services rendered by the Company.
  2. "Gross Revenue" means the total invoiced revenue received by the Company from a Paying Customer introduced by the Representative.
  3. "Net Profit" means the Gross Revenue from a Paying Customer minus all direct and indirect costs attributable to that customer, including but not limited to: (a) payment processing fees and gateway charges; (b) infrastructure and hosting costs; (c) compliance, regulatory, and licensing costs; (d) customer support and onboarding costs; (e) third-party service provider fees; (f) applicable taxes, duties, and levies; (g) any refunds, chargebacks, or credit notes issued to the customer.
  4. "Referred Customer" means a new customer who was first introduced to the Company by the Representative, as verified and acknowledged in writing by both Parties.
  5. "Effective Date" means the date on which both Parties have signed this Agreement.

3. APPOINTMENT AND STATUS

  1. The Company hereby appoints the Representative as an independent sales representative on a non-exclusive basis. The Representative is not an employee, partner, or agent of the Company and shall not represent themselves as such.
  2. The Representative shall have no authority to bind the Company to any agreements, make commitments on behalf of the Company, or accept payments on behalf of the Company.
  3. The Representative shall be solely responsible for all personal tax obligations, including income tax, VAT registration (if applicable), and any other statutory obligations arising from income earned under this Agreement.
  4. This Agreement does not create an employment relationship and the Representative shall not be entitled to any employee benefits, including but not limited to leave, medical aid, pension contributions, or UIF.

4. COMMISSION STRUCTURE

Commission Rate: 15% (Fifteen Percent) of Net Profit
The Representative shall be entitled to a commission equal to fifteen percent (15%) of the Net Profit derived from each Paying Customer referred by the Representative, subject to the conditions set out in this clause.
  1. Eligibility for Commission: Commission shall only become payable when:
    1. The Referred Customer has been verified and acknowledged by the Company in writing;
    2. The Referred Customer has signed a service agreement with the Company;
    3. The Referred Customer has made at least one (1) confirmed payment;
    4. The Company has received and reconciled the payment in full;
    5. All direct and indirect costs attributable to the customer have been deducted to calculate Net Profit.
  2. Payment Schedule: Commission shall be calculated and paid monthly in arrears, within thirty (30) calendar days of the end of each calendar month, provided the minimum threshold of R500 (Five Hundred Rand) in accumulated commission has been reached. If this threshold is not met, commission shall be carried forward to the following month.
  3. Commission Duration: The Representative shall be entitled to commission on a Referred Customer for as long as:
    1. This Agreement remains in force;
    2. The Referred Customer remains an active, paying customer of the Company;
    3. The Representative has not been terminated for breach of this Agreement.
  4. Disputes on Calculation: Should the Representative dispute any commission calculation, they must raise the dispute in writing within fourteen (14) days of receiving the commission statement. Failure to dispute within this period shall constitute acceptance of the calculation.

5. CUSTOMER REFERRAL AND VERIFICATION PROCESS

  1. The Representative must formally introduce each potential customer by submitting a written referral notice to the Company, including the customer's full legal name, business name (if applicable), contact details, and nature of the business opportunity.
  2. The Company shall acknowledge receipt within seven (7) business days and confirm whether the customer is:
    1. Accepted — the customer is new and the referral is approved;
    2. Declined — the customer is already in the Company's pipeline, is an existing customer, or does not meet the Company's onboarding criteria.
  3. Only customers with a confirmed "Accepted" referral status shall qualify for commission.
  4. The Company shall maintain a register of all Referred Customers, which shall be made available to the Representative upon reasonable written request.

6. REPORTING AND TRANSPARENCY

  1. The Company shall provide the Representative with a monthly commission statement containing: (a) a list of all active Referred Customers; (b) Gross Revenue per customer; (c) itemized deductions to arrive at Net Profit; (d) commission payable.
  2. The Representative may, at their own cost and upon thirty (30) days' written notice, request an independent audit of the commission calculations, limited to once per twelve (12) month period.

7. OBLIGATIONS OF THE REPRESENTATIVE

  1. The Representative shall conduct all activities professionally and ethically, in accordance with the Company's values and brand guidelines.
  2. The Representative shall not make any false, misleading, or unauthorized representations about the Company's products, services, pricing, or capabilities.
  3. The Representative shall comply with all applicable laws and regulations, including the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA), the Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act (FAIS), and the Financial Intelligence Centre Act (FICA), where applicable.
  4. The Representative shall not solicit or recruit existing customers of the Company to competing products or services during the term of this Agreement.

8. CONFIDENTIALITY

  1. The Representative acknowledges that they may receive or have access to confidential and proprietary information belonging to the Company, including but not limited to customer data, pricing structures, business strategies, technical infrastructure, and financial information.
  2. The Representative shall not disclose, copy, or use any confidential information for any purpose other than the performance of their duties under this Agreement.
  3. This obligation of confidentiality shall survive the termination of this Agreement indefinitely.
  4. Any breach of this clause shall entitle the Company to immediately terminate this Agreement and pursue legal remedies, including damages.

9. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND BRANDING

  1. The Company grants the Representative a limited, non-transferable, revocable license to use the Company's name, logo, and marketing materials solely for the purpose of introducing potential customers.
  2. The Representative shall obtain prior written approval from the Company before creating or distributing any marketing materials that reference the Company.
  3. All intellectual property rights in the Company's products, services, and materials remain the sole property of the Company.

10. TERM AND TERMINATION

  1. Initial Term: This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue for an initial period of twelve (12) months, whereafter it shall automatically renew for successive twelve (12) month periods unless terminated by either Party.
  2. Termination Without Cause: Either Party may terminate this Agreement by providing sixty (60) calendar days' written notice to the other Party.
  3. Termination With Cause: Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party:
    1. Commits a material breach and fails to remedy such breach within fourteen (14) days of receiving written notice;
    2. Is found guilty of fraud, dishonesty, or gross misconduct;
    3. Becomes insolvent or is placed under business rescue; or
    4. Brings the other Party's name or reputation into disrepute.
  4. Post-Termination Commission: Upon termination without cause, the Representative shall be entitled to receive commission on Referred Customers for a period of three (3) months following the termination date, provided those customers remain active and paying. No commission shall be payable after this three (3) month tail period.
  5. Termination for Breach: In the event of termination for cause by the Company, all commission entitlements shall cease immediately on the date of termination, and no tail period shall apply.

11. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

  1. The Company shall not be liable for any loss, damage, or expense incurred by the Representative in the performance of their duties under this Agreement.
  2. The Representative shall indemnify and hold the Company harmless against all claims, liabilities, damages, and expenses arising from the Representative's actions, omissions, or breach of this Agreement.
  3. The Company makes no guarantees regarding the volume of business, revenue, or profit that may be generated through the Representative's efforts.

12. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

  1. Any dispute arising from or in connection with this Agreement shall first be submitted to negotiation between the Parties in good faith for a period of not less than fourteen (14) days.
  2. Should negotiation fail, the dispute shall be referred to mediation in accordance with the rules of the Arbitration Foundation of Southern Africa (AFSA).
  3. Should mediation fail, the dispute shall be referred to arbitration in accordance with the rules of AFSA, and the decision of the arbitrator shall be final and binding on the Parties.

13. GENERAL PROVISIONS

  1. Governing Law: This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Republic of South Africa.
  2. Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements relating to the subject matter hereof.
  3. Amendment: No amendment or variation of this Agreement shall be valid unless reduced to writing and signed by both Parties.
  4. Severability: If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.
  5. Assignment: The Representative may not assign, cede, or delegate any of their rights or obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the Company.
  6. Notices: All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered via email (with read receipt) or registered mail to the addresses specified above.
  7. Counterparts: This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.
